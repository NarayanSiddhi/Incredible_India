Description

The Umngot River is a scenic river flowing through the northeastern state of Meghalaya in India. What distinguishes the Umngot River, particularly the stretch near the town of Dawki, is its crystal-clear and transparent waters. The river is renowned for its exceptional clarity, allowing visibility of the riverbed and aquatic life up to several meters deep. Dawki, situated on the India-Bangladesh border, has become a popular tourist destination mainly because of the Umngot River. Visitors often enjoy boat rides on the river, where the astonishingly clear water allows them to see the riverbed, rocks, and fish beneath the surface. The surrounding landscape, with lush greenery and hills, enhances the overall picturesque beauty of the Umngot River area. The river serves as a natural border between India and Bangladesh, and its unique clarity has made it a sought-after attraction for nature enthusiasts and tourists exploring Meghalaya.
