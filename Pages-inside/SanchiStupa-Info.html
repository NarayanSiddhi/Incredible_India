Description

Sanchi Stupa, located in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its ancient Buddhist monuments. Built during the Mauryan period, particularly by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, Sanchi Stupa is a symbol of Buddhist architecture and art. The Great Stupa, the main attraction, is a hemispherical dome containing relics of Lord Buddha. The site also features intricately carved gateways known as Toranas, depicting scenes from Buddha's life. Sanchi Stupa stands as a testament to India's rich Buddhist heritage and attracts visitors seeking a glimpse into the country's ancient cultural and religious history.
