Description

Hampi, located in the state of Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rich historical and architectural significance. The city was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th to 16th centuries. Hampi's landscape is dotted with impressive ruins, including intricately carved temples, royal complexes, and other structures that showcase the grandeur of the bygone era. The Virupaksha Temple, Lotus Mahal, and the iconic stone chariot at the Vittala Temple are among the key attractions. The Tungabhadra River adds to the scenic beauty, making Hampi a compelling destination for history enthusiasts and travelers alike.
