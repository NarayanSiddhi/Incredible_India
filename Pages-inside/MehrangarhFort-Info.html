Description

Mehrangarh Fort, located in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, India, is one of the largest forts in the country. Built in the 15th century by Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur, the fort sits atop a 410 feet high hill and offers panoramic views of the Blue City. The fort's architecture showcases a mix of Rajput and Mughal styles, with intricately carved panels, latticed windows, and expansive courtyards. Inside, the fort houses a museum that displays a rich collection of artifacts, including royal palanquins, weapons, paintings, and period costumes. Mehrangarh Fort stands as a symbol of the city's historical grandeur and is a popular tourist destination.
